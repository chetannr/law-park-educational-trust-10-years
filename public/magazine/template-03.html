To Every Child, Every Donor, Every Well-Wisher

Ten years ago, this journey began with a simple belief: every child, no matter where they are born or how much money their family has, deserves a chance to learn.

We started small — one child, one scholarship, one village visit. We drove to Chickaballapur, sat with children and their parents under open skies, and listened to stories of dreams that just needed a little help to come true.

Today, as we mark a decade of this work, we are filled with gratitude. Gratitude for the children who trusted us. Gratitude for the parents who believed us. Gratitude for every donor, volunteer, and partner who said: "Yes, let's do this together."

Our Story

10 Years of Believing in Every Child

The Problem We Decided to Solve

Across India's villages and small towns, millions of children want to go to school. They are bright. They are curious. They are ready to learn. But somewhere between that dream and the classroom, money gets in the way.

Families without enough income are forced to make impossible choices. And quietly, one by one, children drop out — not because they don't want to study, but because nobody stepped in at the right moment.

Law Park Educational Trust was born to be that step.

Our first school visit — Chickaballapur

Who We Are

We are a registered educational trust based in HSR Layout, Bengaluru. Since 2016, we have been travelling to rural areas across India — from Chickaballapur to MM Hills tribal villages — to find children who need partial scholarship support to stay in school.

We don't just send money. We visit in person. We sit with families. We interview children. We pay fees directly to schools so every rupee reaches where it should.

How It Works

1

Identify

School principals, well-wishers, and community members nominate deserving students from income-deprived backgrounds who need support to continue their education.

2

Validate

We visit the location, gather students and parents, and interview each family to verify their situation — with care, respect, and no shortcuts.

3

Embrace

We cover up to 75% of a child's tuition fees. Parents contribute the remaining part — because involvement creates ownership. Anything given fully free has no lasting value.

4

Incubate

Fees are paid directly to the school — not through parents — so children can focus solely on their studies and tap their fullest potential.

Did you know?

What We Do

More Than Scholarships — A Full Circle of Support

When we say we support a child's education, we mean it fully. Here is everything we bring to a child's journey.

🎓 Funded Scholarships

We cover up to 75% of tuition fees for children from income-deprived families — allowing them to stay in school without the constant worry of fees hanging over their heads.

🚗 We Travel to Meet the Kids

Our team travels personally to villages, schools, and communities. We don't wait for children to come to us — we go to them. Face-to-face visits help us understand each child's real situation.

🎒 Essential Supplies Distribution

School bags. Notebooks. Pens. Stationery. Sanitary pads. Drawing kits. We distribute whatever a child needs to walk into school with confidence and dignity.

Did you know?

In 2024, we distributed 200 school bags to tribal school children in MM Hills. In 2025, that number grew to 300.

🎮 Educational Games & Activities

Learning should be joyful. We organise fun, interactive games and activities that make children think, laugh, and discover — because curiosity is the best teacher.

"Sometimes the smallest thing you give a child is the biggest thing they have ever received."

What We Do

Learning, Culture, Careers & Community

📚 Cultural Awareness Programmes

We share stories of language, heritage, and culture — like explaining how the Kannada language evolved through the origin of sounds (Varnamala). Children leave knowing their roots a little better.

💼 Career Counselling

For students in 9th and 10th standard, the future can feel confusing and far away. Our counselling sessions help them see what is possible, understand their options, and make confident choices about their education and career paths.

📖 Library Setup in Rural Schools

We set up libraries in rural schools with donated books — story books, academic texts, and unused notebooks — creating small, quiet corners where imagination can grow.

🤝 Community Events & Talent Showcases

We organise events where children can sing, dance, and show their talents. These moments of celebration remind every child: you are seen, you are valued.

Children's Voices

Three Stories of Hope

Stories inspired by the real children we have met and supported. Names have been changed to protect privacy.

Celebrating learning together

Meena's Story — "I Almost Stopped Going to School"

Meena was twelve when her father lost his daily wage work for two months. The school sent home a reminder about unpaid fees. She sat outside her house, wondering if she would have to stop studying.

Her teacher knew about Law Park Educational Trust. A few weeks later, a team visited her village, met her family, and asked Meena what she wanted to become.

"A teacher," she said quietly.

Her fees were taken care of. She is now in the 9th standard — and attended a career counselling session organised by the Trust. She knows exactly which stream she wants to take after 10th.

Raju's Story — "The School Bag Changed Everything"

Raju lives near MM Hills. Until last year, he carried his books in a torn plastic bag. He was embarrassed to walk into school.

When the Trust visited with 300 school bags, Raju got a new one. It seems like a small thing. But for a ten-year-old boy, that bag meant he belonged — just like every other child. His mother says he now wakes up early every morning, packs his bag the night before, and never misses a day of school.

Sunita's Story — "A Library in My School"

Sunita had never read a storybook. Her school had no library — just textbooks and chalk. When Law Park Educational Trust set up a small library, Sunita picked up a story about a girl who becomes a doctor. She read it three times.

Today she visits the library every week. She wants to be a doctor — because she read about one in a book that someone donated.

"A donated book is not just paper and ink. It is someone's dream, passed on to a child who needed it."
